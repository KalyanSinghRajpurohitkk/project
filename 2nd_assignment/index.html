Biography

"The whole of arithmetic now appeared within the grasp of mechanism."

Charles Babbage (1791-1871) was an English mathematician, philosopher, inventor, and mechanical engineer who is best known for his concept of a programmable computer. His work laid the foundation for modern computing.

Significant Contributions: Babbage originated the idea of a programmable computer with his invention of the **Difference Engine** and **Analytical Engine**. Though his engines were never fully completed during his lifetime, his designs anticipated many elements of modern computing, such as control flow and memory storage.
